Massachusetts Graduation Disparities Analysis

Overall Introduction

Graduating from high school is a major milestone in a student’s life and often reflects academic readiness for college, careers, and other post-secondary opportunities. Massachusetts is one of the strongest-performing states in the country in terms of education, but graduation outcomes are not the same across all schools and districts.

Differences in graduation rates may reflect broader inequalities in educational access, district resources, and student support systems. Some districts consistently report very high graduation rates, while others face lower outcomes that may be associated with higher levels of student need, economic disadvantage, or other demographic factors.

Understanding these disparities is important because graduation is not just an education outcome. It is also connected to long-term economic opportunity, workforce readiness, college access, and social mobility. By examining geographic and social patterns in the data, we can better understand how student outcomes vary across Massachusetts.

To learn more about Massachusetts education data, you can visit the Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education. For district boundary data used in the map, we use information from MassGIS.

Introduction to the Data

Our project uses several datasets from the Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education from 2017 through 2022. These datasets provide school-level information related to student demographics, socioeconomic characteristics, graduation outcomes, SAT performance, and postsecondary attendance.

The individual datasets used in this project are:

Dataset Size Description
Enrollment by Gender 11072 rows × 13 columns Enrollment counts by gender and year
Socioeconomic Data 11055 rows × 15 columns High needs, English learners, economically disadvantaged, and related variables
College Attendance 2397 rows × 13 columns Percent of graduates attending college or university
Graduation Rates 2335 rows × 10 columns Graduation outcomes by school and year
SAT 2171 rows × 7 columns SAT participation and performance measures

These five datasets were manually appended by school and year to create one combined dataset. The combined dataset has a size of 11072 rows and 46 columns. It includes school enrollment demographics, SAT measures, graduation rates, college attendance rates, and socioeconomic factors for schools across a five-year period.

To better match the focus of our project, the full dataset was filtered to only include high schools. This reduces the dataset to approximately 2300 rows and allows us to focus on the schools that directly report graduation-related outcomes. Filtering in this way also makes the final dataset cleaner and more relevant for analyzing graduation disparities.

In addition to the education datasets, we also use school district geographic boundary data from MassGIS. This geographic data allows us to create an interactive map of Massachusetts school districts so that users can explore regional differences in graduation outcomes.

The map below shows graduation rates across Massachusetts districts. Users can change the year using the dropdown and hover over each district to see more detailed district-level information.

In general, the interactive map highlights that graduation outcomes are not evenly distributed across Massachusetts. Some districts consistently report stronger graduation outcomes, while others appear lower, suggesting that place-based and socioeconomic differences may matter.

Summary and Additional Work

In this project, we analyze graduation disparities across Massachusetts school districts and examine how these differences may relate to broader student and district characteristics.

Our interactive map provides one way to explore how graduation rates vary geographically across the state. Additional visualizations will help further explain these patterns and compare graduation outcomes with other education and socioeconomic variables.
